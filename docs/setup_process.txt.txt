Topic Summary

1. Determine the Business Activity and License Type
Begin by identifying the exact nature of your business activity to select the appropriate license category.
Meydan Free Zone offers various licence types, including commercial, service, and industrial. 
This step ensures compliance with free zone regulations and aligns your licence with your business objectives.

2. Reserve Your Trade Name
Select and reserve a unique trade name through the Meydan Free Zone Authority. 
The name must adhere to the naming conventions set by Dubai authorities and should not conflict with existing entities. 
Reservation confirms your chosen name is exclusive to your business and essential for subsequent documentation.

3. Prepare and Submit the Required Documentation
Compile necessary documents, such as a completed application form, passport copies of shareholders and managers, a business plan, and proof of capital if applicable. 
Submit these along with your trade name reservation to Meydan’s licensing department for evaluation and approval.

4. Sign the Legal Documents and Pay Fees
Upon approval, you will be required to sign the Memorandum of Association (MOA) and other relevant legal agreements. 
Concurrently, pay the licensing fees, which typically cover the trade licence, office space (if applicable), and other administrative costs.

5. Obtain Your Business Licence and Establish Your Office
Once all documentation is verified and fees settled, the Meydan Free Zone Authority will issue your business licence. 
Following issuance, if your licence requires physical office space, proceed to lease and set up your premises according to free zone standards, enabling you to commence business operations legally within Dubai.
